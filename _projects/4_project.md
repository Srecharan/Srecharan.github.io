### Introduction
Magnesium alloys are promising materials for biomedical implants due to their lightweight and biocompatibility. However, their high corrosion rate limits their effectiveness in corrosive environments like the human body. This project aimed to improve the corrosion resistance of magnesium alloy microtubes through a surface treatment known as Micro-Arc Oxidation (MAO). The study focused on optimizing key MAO parameters, such as current density, inter-work distance, and coating time, to enhance the durability of the coating under simulated body fluid conditions.

### Methods
As a researcher in this project, I contributed to designing and conducting experiments to optimize the MAO coating on magnesium microtubes. The process involved fabricating microtubes through friction stir back extrusion, followed by the MAO coating application in an alkaline electrolyte solution. A central composite rotatable design was used to adjust parameters like current density and coating time across multiple experimental runs. The microtubes were then subjected to immersion tests in simulated body fluid (SBF) to evaluate corrosion resistance, with changes in weight and surface morphology documented.

### Results
The optimized MAO-coated magnesium microtubes exhibited significantly lower corrosion rates and improved coating adhesion compared to untreated samples. By adjusting current density and coating time, a dense, uniform ceramic layer was achieved, enhancing corrosion resistance. Porosity levels decreased, leading to improved mechanical integrity and durability in corrosive environments. The MAO-coated tubes retained up to 90% of their structural integrity over a 12-day immersion period in SBF, while uncoated tubes exhibited severe pitting and degradation.

### Discussion
The findings confirm that optimized MAO parameters can effectively enhance the corrosion resistance of magnesium alloy microtubes, making them more suitable for biomedical applications. The improved coating protects the material from corrosive elements, extending the lifespan of magnesium-based implants. Future work will focus on refining the coating process to further reduce porosity and examining the long-term effects of MAO coatings under various physiological conditions.
